How NYC got a little more red in the 2024 Election

Voter data shows the red wave was more of a blue dip

By Erin Maher

December 1, 2024

Last month, the United States of America blushed red during the national election when Donald Trump, the 45th President of the United States, was re-elected - and won both the popular vote and the electoral college vote. The red wave continued the ride as the GOP also gained control of the Senate and maintained control of the House of Representatives. Trump not only managed to capture every swing state, but he also flipped six states that voted Democratic in 2020.

New York City, a Democratic stronghold, remained blue but saw Trump making gains in every borough since the 2020 election.

In the Bronx for example, where Trump made a 26% increase in votes between 2020 and 2024, Democratic candidate Kamala Harris lost 31% of the votes that Joe Biden had in the Bronx in 2020. In total, across New York City, Trump gained 94,612 votes between 2020 and 2024, while Harris lost 573,619 votes that Biden received in 2020.
